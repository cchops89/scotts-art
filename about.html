The Artist

About Scott Carle

A physician, a painter, and a lifelong student of the natural world — creating vivid botanicals that bloom from darkness.

His Story

Painting since the age of nine

Scott Carle has been painting since the age of nine, inspired by his grandfather A.P. Finken — a celebrated artist known for his religious and wildlife paintings. That early exposure to the discipline and beauty of oil painting planted a seed that never stopped growing.

By day, Scott has practiced Occupational Medicine in Central Arkansas for over 35 years. By choice, he paints. His signature: a black canvas, then vivid, dimensional flowers that seem to grow out of the darkness.

His work blends a scientist's eye for structure with an artist's complete freedom. No two paintings are alike. No flower is ordinary.

The Inspiration

A.P. Finken

Before Scott ever picked up a brush, there was his grandfather — Andrew Peter “Pete” Finken. A German immigrant who settled in Stuttgart, Arkansas, Pete worked as a machinist by trade but was an artist by nature.

In retirement, Pete devoted himself fully to painting — religious scenes, wildlife, and landscapes that captured the beauty of the Arkansas Delta. His talent earned deep admiration in the community: the city of Stuttgart honored him with an official A.P. Pete Finken Day, and his work is preserved at the Museum of the Grand Prairie.

It was watching Pete paint as a nine-year-old that lit the spark in Scott. That early apprenticeship — learning to see light, mix color, and work with oil — became the foundation for everything that followed. Three generations later, the tradition continues.
